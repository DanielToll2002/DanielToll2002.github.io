Introduction

In 1830, Greece claimed its independence from the Ottoman Empire. Throughout much of the late 19th century and the early 20th century, Greece added numerous islands and territories to the continental country. Most of these included Greek-speaking populations.

Throughout World War II, Greece was invaded by Italy and ultimately occupied by Germany from 1941 through 1944 . During this time, fighting continued in the area amidst a civil war that consisted of supporters of the king and other anti-communist and communist rebels.

After the war in 1952, Greece joined NATO. A military dictatorship was then established in 1967 that halted the political liberties of the citizenry and forced the king to escape the country. The dictatorship collapsed in 1974 and democratic elections abolished the monarchy. The newly formulated parliamentary republic still stands to this day. Greece joined what is now referred to as the EU in 1981, and became the 12th member of the EMU twenty years later.

Geography

Location

Greece is located in Southern Europe between Albania and Turkey. It borders the Aegean Sea, Ionian Sea, and the Mediterranean Sea.

Climate

Greece has a temperate, mild climate with wet winters, but hot and dry summers.

Terrain

Throughout the country, vast mountain ranges extend into the sea. They form peninsulas and small chains of islands.

Government

Government Type

Greece is considered a parliamentary republic.

Constitution

There have been several constitutions in the past that have been used as the governing document of Greece. The most recent one was put into effect on June 11, 1975 and has been amended three times - in 1986, 2001, and 2008.

Amendments:

  • Must be proposed by a minimum of 50 members of Parliament.
  • Must be agreed upon by a three-fifths majority vote across two separate ballots that are at least 30 days apart.
  • Amendment passage requires an absolute majority vote by the next elected Parliament.
  • An amendment that is entered into force must be finalized through a "special parliamentary resolution".
  • Articles that concern human rights, freedoms and the form of government cannot be amended.
